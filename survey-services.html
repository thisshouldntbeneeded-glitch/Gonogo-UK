Survey types we build

Different questions answer different business problems. We help you choose the right format, timing and channel for each use case [web:114][web:116][web:119].

CSAT surveys

Customer Satisfaction surveys are best used after a specific touchpoint such as a purchase, booking, delivery, appointment or support case, when you want clear feedback on that single interaction [web:114][web:118][web:126].

NPS surveys

Net Promoter Score surveys are more useful for measuring overall loyalty and brand sentiment across a wider customer base, usually on a periodic basis rather than after every transaction [web:114][web:118][web:124].

CES surveys

Customer Effort Score surveys work well after service or support processes where the real question is how easy or difficult it was for the customer to get something done [web:116][web:118][web:123].

Post-purchase feedback

These surveys help you identify fulfilment, handover, packaging, product-fit or first-use issues soon after a purchase, before they become complaints or lost repeat revenue [web:117][web:69].

Onboarding surveys

Useful in the first days or weeks of a relationship, onboarding surveys show whether new customers understand what to do next, where they are getting stuck and what support they still need [web:115][web:119].

Churn and cancellation surveys

Triggered when customers leave or go quiet, these surveys surface the real reasons behind churn and can help shape retention and win-back actions [web:122].

Website feedback surveys

Website widgets and intercept surveys help you understand whether visitors found what they needed, why they did not convert and where the journey is creating doubt or friction [web:117][web:119].

In-location QR surveys

Ideal for stores, clinics, venues, restaurants and service points, QR surveys make it easy to collect feedback at the moment the experience is still fresh [web:115][web:117].

Bespoke survey programmes

For more complex journeys, we can create custom survey flows around complaints, recurring service reviews, account management, product feedback and wider customer-experience tracking [web:115][web:119].

Choosing the right metric

We help clients choose the right survey type for the business question they actually need answered, whether that is loyalty, satisfaction or effort [web:114][web:116][web:125].

Use NPS when

You want a broader read on customer loyalty, future advocacy and overall brand sentiment, typically across a wider audience at planned intervals [web:114][web:124][web:126].

Use CSAT when

You need a clear score on a specific touchpoint such as a call, delivery, appointment, support case or completed transaction [web:114][web:118][web:126].

Use CES when

You want to understand how easy or difficult customers found a process, especially in support, issue resolution, onboarding or account tasks [web:116][web:118][web:123].
